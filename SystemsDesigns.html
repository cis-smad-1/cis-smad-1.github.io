Systems Designs and Prototypes

Basic Requirements

Overview

Create an information system that will support the requirements in order to create a functional and aesthetically pleasing story analyzer website

Major Project Goals

  • Updated Documentation
  • All source code for the interfaces and processed in the correct order
  • Well-formatted and professional
  • Documentation on how to set up and use the finished systems

Obstacles / Potential Technical Issues

SMAD

  • Displaying the Graphics
  • Making the UX accessible to users
  • Fitting the information in the screen width

CIS

  • Coding something that we have no experience with

Audience(Personas)

Given Audience: Researchers, historians, reporters, journalists, professors
  • Persona 1: Grad Student (22-28)
    • Goals: Finding information quickly in research
    • Frustrations: Having to go to multiple websites to find documents, looking for keywords

  • Persona 2: Lawyer (30-70)
    • Goals: Analyzing law documents
    • Frustrations: Having to go to multiple sources for documents, not being able to find specific cases, looking for keywords, finding people’s names

Advanced Requirements

UX Research/ Testing

  • We will be conducting interviews with non-users with no prior knowledge of the site and asking them to perform a series of tasks with no/ minimal guidance. The questions and tasks we will ask of the interviewees will be as follows:
    • Locate the Trump Tower Moscow Project pages from the Mueller Report.
    • Find the amount of times the word “investigation” appears in the House Impeachment Report- Exec Summary Section 1.
    • What date is mentioned the most in the House Impeachment Report- Exec Summary Section 1?
    • In what line is Rudy Guiliani’s name first mentioned in the House Impeachment Report- Exec Summary Section 1?
  • The people being interviewed will be asked to think out loud and vocalize all frustrations they face throughout their experience using story analyzer. This will ensure that we will be able to understand where exactly the frustrations lie and what the needs of the user are.
